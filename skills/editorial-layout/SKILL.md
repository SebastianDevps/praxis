> Curated from open-design editorial.

## Typographic Rhythm

- **Measure**: 60–75 characters per line (including spaces) — narrower fatigues mobile; wider loses the eye
- **Line-height**: 1.6–1.7 for body; 1.2–1.3 for headings
- **Body size**: 17–19px on desktop; 16px floor on mobile
- **Serif for editorial body is acceptable** (unlike dashboards); Georgia, Lora, Source Serif work well at these sizes

## Reading Flow

- Paragraph length: 3–6 sentences; hard limit 8
- No orphan headings — a heading needs at least 2 lines of body text beneath it before a break
- Drop-cap or lead paragraph for long-form openers — signals "start here"
- Avoid centered body text; reserve centering for pull quotes and captions

## Section Pacing

Break long sections with rhythm tools:

| Tool | When to use |
|------|-------------|
| Pull quote | Highlight the single most shareable line in a section |
| Subheading | Every 300–400 words, not decorative |
| Image / figure | Interrupts a wall of text; must add information, not fill space |
| Horizontal rule | Signals a topic shift, not a section within the same topic |

## Pull Quotes

- Extract, don't invent — the quote must exist verbatim in the body
- 1.3–1.5× body size, lighter weight or italic, with visible margin offset
- Max 2 pull quotes per 1000 words

## Spacing Hierarchy

- H1: 2–3× body margin-bottom
- H2: 1.5–2× body margin-bottom, half that margin-top
- H3 and below: 1× body margin, treated as a run-in lead

## Code and Footnotes

- Inline code: monospace, soft background, no bold — contrast alone is sufficient
- Footnotes: bottom of article or expandable on click, not mid-paragraph — footnote markers break reading rhythm
