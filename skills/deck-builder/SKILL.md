## Structure Before Slides

Define the narrative arc first — slide count comes last:

1. **Hook** — the problem or tension that makes the audience care
2. **Context** — what's true today (data, situation)
3. **Insight** — the non-obvious thing you've learned
4. **Solution** — what you're proposing
5. **Evidence** — why it works (proof, metrics, precedents)
6. **Ask** — the single thing you want from this audience

Skipping to solution before earning context is the most common deck failure.

## One Idea Per Slide

Each slide has one claim. The title IS the claim ("Revenue grew 40% YoY"), not a topic label ("Revenue"). If you need a second bullet, it's a second slide.

## Visual Hierarchy

- Title: largest, left-aligned or centered, max 2 lines
- Supporting visual or stat: dominant; takes 50–70% of slide area
- Body copy: supporting detail only; max 25 words per text block
- No more than 3 visual layers on one slide

## Minimal Text

Convert prose bullets to one of:
- A single large number + a label
- A before/after comparison
- A visual (chart, diagram, screenshot)

If the text can't be cut, it belongs in the speaker notes.

## Grid Consistency

Pick one layout grid (e.g., 12-col, 16px baseline) and hold it across all slides. Inconsistent spacing signals an unfinished deck.

## Speaker Notes vs. Slide Text

Slide text = what the audience reads. Speaker notes = what you say. They should not be the same. The slide is the backdrop; you are the presenter.

## Transitions

None by default. Add only when a transition communicates sequence (step 1 → 2) or reveals a comparison. Decorative wipes reduce credibility.
